Crossed Gage Study

This video will cover the Gage Crossed Repeatability and Reproducibility study, which is a balanced study designed to measure variation from three sources: the Measurement Device (variation from repeatability), the Operator, the Parts*Operator (variation from reproducibility), and Parts (variation from the different Parts used).

Start this tutorial with the app Gage Study installed. If you have not installed the app, see Apps for Origin to find and install the app.

Note: Since Origin 2025b, this app has been preshipped and you can open it by selecting Statistics: Quality Improvement> Gage Study... menu

Steps

Click the app icon in the Apps Gallery window, or select Statistics: Quality Improvement> Gage Study... menu to open the Gage Study tool.

Create a Gage Worksheet

  1. Click on Create Gage Worksheet to open the dialog.
  2. In the opened dialog, enter 10 for Number of Parts, 3 for Number of Operators, and 2 for Number of Replicates.
  3. There are three randomization options. 1) Do not randomize – Under this option, run order will be the same as standard order; 2) Randomize all runs – Under this option, all runs will be randomized across both parts and operators; 3) Randomize runs within operators – Under this option, Operator 1 will be assigned runs 1-10 and 31-40, Operator 2, runs 11-20 and 41-50, and Operator 3, runs 21-30 and 51-60. Part numbers within these assigned runs will be randomized.
  4. Click on Book1 to show results under options 2) and 3). Double click the filter cell under RunOrder to modify the rule and explore the differences between the two approaches.
  5. We will select option 3) for our Crossed R&R Gage Study. Enter the corresponding Measurements under column E.
